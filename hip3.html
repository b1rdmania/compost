Why This Exists

The market has stats. It still needs memory.

A lot of HIP-3 data already exists in fragments. What does not exist cleanly is a public record that explains what changed, why it mattered, and which product lessons builders should actually take seriously.

Compost originally looked at HIP-3 through the lens of capital formation. The more useful insight now is simpler: builder markets are becoming businesses, and public analysis around them is still immature. Volume alone is not the story. Fees, launch timing, concentration, category selection, distribution, and market quality all shape what builders can sustainably own.

This page is the first step in a public intelligence layer for HIP-3. No closed data room. No private dashboards. Just a sharper public read on what builder markets are doing, what the leaders are teaching the rest of the ecosystem, and where the next wave might come from.

Three Durable Lessons

What HIP-3 has already made clear.

The most useful public analysis is not a leaderboard by itself. It is a model for understanding why some builders break out while others never really convert curiosity into durable flow.

The rails are real

HIP-3 is no longer a thought experiment. The category has real volume, real fees, real open interest, and enough surviving builders to justify dedicated public coverage.

Distribution beats theory

The winning builder story so far is not "best architecture." It is "best packaging of a behavior people immediately understand." Equities, commodities, and indices made HIP-3 legible.

Fee share creates real incentives

A fixed 50% builder fee share means market selection and execution matter. If builders are earning on the flow they create, then public business analysis becomes a core part of the ecosystem.

The key product lesson

Public data is enough to show that HIP-3 is no longer a novelty. What it still needs is better framing. The opportunity is not only to count volume. It is to explain which market categories are converting into real businesses, how concentrated the ecosystem is becoming, and what the builders who are actually winning are doing differently.

What this page is not

This is not a trading frontend, not a private data terminal, and not a pretend proprietary dashboard. It is a public research layer for people who care about HIP-3 and want something more useful than scattered screenshots and isolated charts.
