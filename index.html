Our story

The September 2025 litepaper captured the original concept. Below is how that experiment played out—and what remains on this site.

What it was

The original concept (Sept 2025)

The litepaper spelled out a Hyperliquid-native vault: pool HYPE toward builder-market economics, capture fee exposure from HIP-3 (and later HIP-4) routes, wrap it in one receipt token (cHYPE)—full mechanics and custody narrative on paper.

Why it didn’t land

Complexity, not missing polish

Builder fees don’t map to a single clean LP story—growth mode, wrappers, deployer economics, and counterparty / default risk stack up. The model only felt obvious to people who already deeply understood HIP-3 markets; everyone else hit a wall.

What it is now

Experiment in concept & craft

As a product raise, it’s parked—but the work isn’t erased: testnet demos, HIP intelligence boards, ecosystem stats, and honest writing. Same curiosity, without pretending a live vault is here.

Why Hyperliquid

Decentralised, on-chain, and built for serious volume—perps and builder-deployed markets on one stack.

HIP-3 lets teams launch their own perp markets; deployers earn a protocol-defined share of fees. Read the HIP-3 spec →

HIP-4 adds outcome-style markets—early, mostly testnet, still evolving. Our HIP-4 notes →
